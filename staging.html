1.2. Opener’s rebid

Following a transfer to a major, opener accepts at the 1-level with 3-card support and anything less than a GF.

With 4-card support, opener raises as though responder had bid 1M.

Otherwise, opener makes normal rebid.

1.2.1. When transfer is doubled

 • opener tries to make normal bid
 • XX = four-cards in transfer suit
 • pass = three-cards in transfer suit   ➤  responder can XX to play (no rescue)

4. 1m – 2H = 11–12 bal.

4.1. Advantages

 • enables 1m–2NT = 13-15 or 18+   and 1m–3NT = 16-17

 • opener can show both-minors-minimum   and both-minors-with-extras (forcing)

 • allows NT to be played from either side

4.2. Disadvantages

 • must drop responder’s reverse Flannery

 • opps can double 2♥ to show hearts

 • extras system to learn
