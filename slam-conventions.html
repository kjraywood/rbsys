Picture Jumps

2/1 Auctions

  Fast Arrival vs. Picture Bidding,     Part 1, Part 2, Part 3     –  Andrew Gumperz

Responder may initially conceal 4-card support for opener’s major when intending to make a picture jump to game on the second round. It is highly unlikely that the auction will be too high for responder to jump on the second round.

We do not conceal 4-card support in other cases because a jump to 3M may not be available on the 2nd-round (e.g.  2M or 2NT rebid by opener). This is contrary to the recommendation of Gumperz (part 1, page 2, hand 2).

Minorwood

4m is minorwood if:
 ●  minor-suit agreed below 4m   (incl. via splinter)
 ●  neither opponent bid on preceeding round
 ●  4m bidder is unlimited or has been   invited back into the auction

With some specific exceptions, 4m is not minorwood when it is the first call that shows support for partner’s minor.

If a minor fit is first discovered at the 4-level, and it is not one of the specific exceptions, then 4 of the cheapest unbid-unbid suit is key-card asking.

Optional minorwood:
4m shows slam interest. To accept, partner bids 4m+1 to ask for KC. To decline, partner shows KCs beginning at 4m+2.

4-level agreement

The KC-asking bid depends on the number of unbid suits.

 ●  two ⇒ lower unbid is KC
 ●  one ⇒ unbid suit is KC,   except if clubs
 ●  zero ⇒ fourth suit is KC, except if clubs

If diamonds are agreed and clubs is the fourth or unbid suit, then the cheaper of each player’s !2nd-suit! is KC asking.

First-round Exclusion

First-round jumps to 4M are to play.

Other first-round jumps that are one-level higher than a splinter are exclusion.

Specific Kings

 ●  King-ask = 5T + 1 (no exceptions)   ➤  Promises that we have all the key-cards   ➤  if trumps ≠ ♠, then 5NT ⇒ K of 5T + 1

Responding to the Queen Ask

Without the queen, teller returns to the trump suit

With the queen but no side kings, teller makes the cheapest bid other than the trump-suit

With the queen and one or more side kings, teller shows specific kings up-the-line

If the cheapest non-trump-suit bid (denying any side kings) is a suit, then 5NT is used to show the king in that suit

Interference

We ignore doubles of any asking or telling bids. i.e.  we bid as though the double did not occur.

 ●  D0P1 below 5T-1 where   ➤  0 ⇒ 0 or 3   ➤  1 ⇒ 1 or 4  ●  DEPO otherwise
