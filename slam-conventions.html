19. Picture Jumps

19.1. Two-over-one auctions

  Fast Arrival vs. Picture Bidding,     Part 1, Part 2, Part 3     –  Andrew Gumperz

Responder may initially conceal 4-card support for opener’s major when intending to make a picture jump to game on the second round. It is highly unlikely that the auction will be too high for responder to jump on the second round.

We do not conceal 4-card support in other cases because a jump to 3M may not be available on the 2nd-round (e.g.  2M or 2NT rebid by opener). So, contrary to the recommendation of Gumperz (part 1, page 2, hand 2), the 2nd-round jump to 3M shows strong slam-interest but denies 4-card support.

20. General Principles for RKC

Four of a previously bid major is natural. However, we use 4♠ as kickback for hearts when we have agreed hearts and it when it is not a picture jump.

20.4. When 4NT is Selfwood

In suit-only auctions, asking for key-cards in your own suit (selfwood) is often not possible.

4NT can be used as selfwood when:
 ●  you have bid only one suit
 ●  a forcing-2NT is available   (so a natural 4NT is not needed)
 ●  you can agree partner’s suit(s) with a   below-game forcing-bid

In the auction below, 4NT is not KC for spades because they can be set as trump with a GF 3♠. 4NT is not needed as natural because a 2NT would be forcing. Responder has bid only one suit hence 4NT is selfwood in that suit.

21.3. Three-level non-agreement

The general principle is that for 4m to be minorwood, we must first explicitely agree the minor-suit, or set it as trumps with a transfer after a notrump opening.

So when opener or responder bids a minor at the 3-level, a raise to 4m is just forward going (forcing).

When the auction is at 3m without agreement, the KC-asking bid is a jump to a suit-bid below 4NT. In priority order the KC-asking suit is:

 ●  cheapest unbid-suit  ●  fourth suit  ●  opener’s 2nd-suit  ●  responder’s 2nd-suit

22. 4NT in high-level competition

  Added Sep 15, 2018

  ACBL district-6 panel – Feb/Mar 2011

22.1. Standard

We open one-major, they preempt ➤  4NT = KC asking

We open one-minor, they preempt
 ●  4NT = take-out ∼ 4 × m + 6 × om#

We overcall @ 1-level, they preempt
 ●  4NT = take-out

22.2. Other Suggestions

22.2.1. Mike Lawrence

We overcall two-minor, they preempt ➤  4NT = plain aces

22.2.2. Eric Greco

We act, they preempt ➤  if partner has shown five or more, except for 1-level overcalls, then 4NT is KC asking.

23. First-round ace-asking

23.1. Plain Blackwood

1suit – 4NT = plain Blackwood

23.2. Gerber

 ●  Only applies after 1NT and 2NT openings  ●  Responses: 03, 14, 2 & min, 2 & max

Self splinters (not Gerber) after NT rebids

23.3. Cheap Key-Card after preempts

 ●  we open a preempt  ●  next hand passes or doubles for take-out  ●  (4♦ / 3♣) | (4♣ / other ) = key-card ask  ●  Responses: 0, 1-, 1+, 2-, 2+

23.4. First-round Exclusion

First-round jumps to 4M are to play.

Other first-round jumps that are one-level higher than a splinter are exclusion.

Responses: 0, 1, 2-, 2+, 3-, 3+

24. KC Responses

03, 14, 2−, 2+

24.1. Specific Kings

 ●  King-ask = 5T + 1 (no exceptions)
  ➤  Promises that we have all the key-cards
  ➤  if trumps ≠ ♠, then 5NT ⇒ K of 5T + 1

24.2. Responding to the Queen Ask

Without the queen, teller returns to the trump suit

With the queen but no side kings, teller makes the cheapest bid other than the trump-suit

With the queen and one or more side kings, teller shows specific kings up-the-line

If the cheapest non-trump-suit bid (denying any side kings) is a suit, then 5NT is used to show the king in that suit

24.3. Interference

We ignore doubles of any asking or telling bids. i.e.  we bid as though the double did not occur.

 ●  D0P1 below 5T-1,   Dbl = 0 or 3, P = 1 or 4
 ●  DEPO otherwise

25. Exclusion

Applies when:

 ●  direct triple-jump response to 1M opening  ●  suit agreed, then jump
 ●  a non-jump following   ➤  a control-bid by partner   ➤  suit agreement at 4-level

Responses: 0, 1, 2-, 2+, 3-, 3+
