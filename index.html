Part 1: Summary

General Approach

 •  1NT = 15 to 17  •  2NT = 20 to bad 22  •  Five-card majors  •  2/1 GF  •  semi-forcing 1NT  •  aggressive preempts and pre-balancing  •  low-level take-out doubles

Responding to 1♣

Responder bypasses 1♦ when:
 •  can cope with a three-card raise  •  will rebid or raise NT

1NT Opening

Stayman

 •  Promises a 4-card major  •  Includes 5×♠ + 5×other  inv

Rebids: splinter  •  Smolen @ 2-level  •  OMaR [ 5×♠ + 5×m inv  |  COG (3NT = 4333 with fit)  |  void splinter  |  set trump for RKC ]

We do NOT play delayed Texas or delayed Gerber

vs double: pass promises a stopper  •  reverse transfers  •  2NT = 4-4 majors min  •  3♣ = 4-4 majors max  •  Redouble = double neg. (no stop, no M)

Jacoby transfers

Super accept = 2T + 1   ‣  Responder can re-transfer or describe further
Transfer then 3m = GF   ‣  Opener assumes COG. New suit agrees minor
OMaR [ ♥ + m, inv  |  COG (3NT vs. 4M)  |  void self-splinter ]

Minor-suit transfers

2♠  = ♣ weak|strong, or balanced inv   ‣  Opener bids 2NT (min) or 3♣ (max).
2NT = ♦ weak|strong, or weak with both minors   ‣  Opener bids preferred minor

Transfer to ♣ then 3♦ = 5-5 minors GF.   ‣  then by opener: 3♥ ⇒ ♣, 3♠ ⇒ ♦

Transfer to minor then 3M = shortness

3-level responses

3♣ = five-card Stayman
3♦ = at least 5-5 majors, GF
3♥,3♠ = stiff with three in other major

One-major overcalls

 •  2NT = good 4-card raise  •  cue = good 3-card raise, or any force  •  Jump cue = mixed raise  •  double of raise = responsive  •  double of new suit = snapdragon

Part 2: Details

Opening and responding

1NT = 15 to 17, 2NT = 20 to bad 22, Five-card majors, 2/1 GF, semi-forcing 1NT, aggressive preempts and pre-balancing, most low-level doubles are take-out.

Opening bids

Balanced hands

Pass with 12 HCP, 4333 and poor spots. Open other 12-counts and some 11-counts with good spots.

Unbalanced hands

Open with as little as 9 or 10 HCP, lots of playing strength and two quick tricks. e.g. Open 1♠ with: ♠AJTxxx ♥x ♦Axx ♣xx

Third seat

A good four-card major is possible. We do NOT open light in 3rd. Weak twos and weak jump-overcalls are often a decent five-card suit when NV.

Responding to 1♣

After 1♣-1M;2M responder can not introduce diamonds to play. So responder bypasses 1♦ when able to cope with a three-card raise, and without intention to introduce diamonds as a possible strain.
