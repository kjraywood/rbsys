3.4. Vs double of Stayman

In order of priority:
 • 3♣ = 4-4 majors max  • 2NT = 4-4 majors min
 • pass promises a stopper   (may have a stack)
 • reverse transfers deny stopper   (2♦ = 4 × ♥, 2♥ = 4 × ♠)
 • Redble = double neg. (no stop, no M)
  ➤  scramble for a place to play,    Smolen is OFF

3.4.1. When opener passes

Responder can redouble as re‑Stayman, but must have at least two clubs and at least invitational values because opener might have a stack and be intending to pass.

With a garbage-Stayman hand (three-suiter short in clubs), responder bids 2♦ and opener corrects with a four-card major.

With short-clubs and a GF, responder bids 3♣ as re‑Stayman. Smolen is ON.

Other bids are as though opener had replied to Stayman with 2♦, although in this case, opener may still have a four-card major. It’s possible to miss a 4‑-4 major-suit fit when responder rebids 2NT and opener passes.

3.6. Vs double of transfer

The scheme is similar to when Stayman is doubled. The focus is on a stopper in the doubled suit.

 • four-card support makes a super accept
 • accepting transfer   = 3-card support, A or K of doubled suit
 • pass promises a stopper
 • redouble = no stopper

Our super-accept scheme is ON, except that opener may use a reverse transfer to make responder declare.

Anti-splinter super-accepts (showing values, four-card-support and minumum) neither confirm nor deny control. A subsequent 3NT by responder shows control of the transfer-suit and is a slam try with preference to declare.

3.7. Minor-suit transfers

 • 2♠ = ♣, or balanced inv
  ➤  2NT = min   ➤  3♣ = max
 • 2NT = ♦, any strength
  ➤  3♣ = pre-decline   ➤  3♦ = pre-accept (♦Qxx or better)

3.7.1. Rebids with strong hands

 • new-suit = shortness   ➤  opener assumes COG
 • 4m = minorwood  • other jump-shift = exclusion  • 4NT = slam inv.

A 3NT rebid is generally to play, but opener may continue with undisclosed extras following a previous-round min or pre-decline.

3.8. Interference over 1NT

Texas is ON when the interference is below  2NT.

When the interference is 2NT or higher, we treat the situation similarly to when they make a preemptive opening-bid in that strain.

3.8.1. Vs penalty double

 • pass = to play   ➤  Redbl by opener = 5-card suit
 • Redbl = ♣ or ♦
 • 2♣ = majors   ➤   2♦ or Redbl = no pref 
 • 2♦, 2♥ = transfers  • 2♠ = 4 × ♠ + 6 × m  • 2NT = ♣ + ♦  • 3m = 6 × m + 4 × ♥

3.8.2. Vs natural 2C (single suit)

 • dble = take-out   ➤  subsequent cue (3♣) requests stopper
 • other system ON

3.8.3. Vs artificial double or 2C

Includes 2♣ = ♣ + other (known or unknown)

 • System ON  • double of 2♣ = Stayman

We can pass to discover an unspecified suit, then use our normal agreements vs interference (take-out double, Unified Lebensohl).

3.8.4. Vs 2D, 2H or 2S (any)

Double = negative
  Countering Notrump Interference     –  Phillip Martin
 • take-out of bid suit   as though the interference was natural
 • creates force to 2♠   ➤  subsequent doubles are penalty

2M is to play except when the opps have promised at least five in the suit (e.g.  they play transfers vs our 1NT); then it is Michaels.

Other bids are Unified Lebensohl

3.8.5. Vs 2NT (minors)

Our defence to 2NT intervention is similar to our defence to a 2NT opening that shows both minors. However, when a 2NT opening is doubled, we play the next double as take-out because we have not established the balance of power. After 1NT-(2NT)-X we are assumed to have the balance of power and subsequent doubles are penalty.

 • pass = nothing or penalty of both suits   ➤  subsequent double = penalty
 • X = values   ➤  next double = penalty
 • 3♣ = both majors weak or strong  • 3♦ = both majors medium  • 3♥, 3♠ = competitive (NF, mild inv.)  • 3NT = to play
 • 4♣, 4♦ = Slammish in corresponding major   (∼Namyats)
 • 4♥, 4♠ = to play

3.8.6. Vs 3m

  Unusual treatment. 3⟨unbid-suit⟩ = NF.

At the 4-level, we use (non-)leaphing-Michaels; similar to our defence to natural 2m or 3m openings.

 • X = negative  • 3⟨unbid-suit⟩ = natural, competitive, NF  • 3NT = to play  • 4♣ = om + unknown M  • 4♦ = majors  • 4♥, 4♠ = to play

3.8.7. Vs 3M

Similar to vs their opening 3M, we play non-leaping michaels except that 4NT is natural; not both minors-weak.

 • X = negative  • 3♠ (M=♥) = natural, NF  • 3NT = to play  • 4m = m + oM  • 4oM = to play  • 4M = both minors, decent hand  • 4NT = natural inv.
