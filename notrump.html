3. 1NT Opening

After a Jacoby transfer, we use 2nd-round transfers.

  • transfer to spades then 2NT shows clubs

  • All invitational hands with five spades use Stayman

  • Stayman promises a 4-card major

3.2.3. Vs double of Stayman

In order of priority:
 ●  3♣ = 4-4 majors max  ●  2NT = 4-4 majors min
 ●  pass promises a stopper   (may have a stack)
 ●  reverse transfers deny stopper   (2♦ = 4 × ♥, 2♥ = 4 × ♠)
 ●  Redble = double neg. (no stop, no M)   ➤  scramble for a place to play,    Smolen is OFF

  Modified Aug 27, 2018

When opener passes, showing a stopper, we can often get back to our normal auction. Responder can redouble as re‑Stayman, but avoids this with a singleton club because opener might have a stack and be intending to pass.

With short-clubs and a GF, responder bids 3♣ as re-Stayman. Smolen is ON.

With short-clubs invitational strength and less than five spades (otherwise 2♠) responder bids 2NT which is usually at least 4-3 in the majors. This may miss a 4-4 major fit when opener passes, but otherwise, opener can jump to game in a 5-card major, or show a 4-card major.

3.3. Jacoby transfers

3.3.1. Anti-splinter super-accepts

  Unusual treatment

With four-card support and a max (a classic super-accept) we jump accept immediately.

With four-card support and less than a max, we show the location of outside values. To ensure that a re-transfer is always possible, opener uses 2NT to show values in suit of transfer.

3.6. Minor-suit transfers

 ●  2♠ = ♣, or balanced inv
  ➤  2NT = min   ➤  3♣ = max
 ●  2NT = ♦, any strength
  ➤  3♣ = pre-decline   ➤  3♦ = pre-accept (♦Qxx or better)

3.6.1. Rebids with strong hands

 ●  new-suit = shortness   ➤  opener assumes COG
 ●  4m = minorwood  ●  other jump-shift = exclusion  ●  4NT = slam inv.

A 3NT rebid is generally to play, but opener may continue with undisclosed extras following a previous-round min or pre-decline.

3.8. Interference over 1NT

3.8.1. Vs penalty double

 ●  pass = to play   ➤  Redbl by opener = 5-card suit
 ●  Redbl = ♣ or ♦
 ●  2♣ = majors   ➤   2♦ or Redbl = no pref 
 ●  2♦, 2♥ = transfers  ●  2♠ = 4 × ♠ + 6 × m  ●  2NT = ♣ + ♦  ●  3m = 6 × m + 4 × ♥

3.8.2. Vs natural 2C (single-suit)

 ●  dble = take-out   ➤  subsequent cue (3♣) requests stopper
 ●  other system ON

3.8.3. Vs artificial double or 2C

 ●  System ON  ●  double of 2♣ = Stayman

We can pass to discover an unspecified suit, then use a take-out double or Lebensohl.

3.8.4. Vs 2NT (minors)

We play our 2NT system

3.8.5. Vs 2D, 2H or 2S (any)

 ●  2M = to play   (except Michaels vs 5-cd suit)
 ●  3m = inv.  ●  3M = forcing  ●  3 cue-bid = Stayman, no stopper  ●  2NT = Lebensohl
