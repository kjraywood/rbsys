3. 1NT Opening

After a Jacoby transfer, we use 2nd-round transfers.

  • transfer to spades then 2NT shows clubs

  • All invitational hands with five spades use Stayman

  • Stayman promises a 4-card major

3.3. Vs double of Stayman

In order of priority:
 ●  3♣ = 4-4 majors max  ●  2NT = 4-4 majors min
 ●  pass promises a stopper   (may have a stack)
 ●  reverse transfers deny stopper   (2♦ = 4 × ♥, 2♥ = 4 × ♠)
 ●  Redble = double neg. (no stop, no M)
  ➤  scramble for a place to play,    Smolen is OFF

3.3.1. When opener passes

Responder can redouble as re‑Stayman, but must have at least two clubs and at least invitational values because opener might have a stack and be intending to pass.

With a garbage-Stayman hand (three-suiter short in clubs), responder bids 2♦ and opener corrects with a four-card major.

With short-clubs and a GF, responder bids 3♣ as re‑Stayman. Smolen is ON.

Other bids are though opener had replied to Stayman with 2♦, although in this case, opener may still have a four-card major. It’s possible to miss a 4‑-4 major-suit fit when responder rebids 2NT and opener passes.

3.5. Vs double of transfer

The scheme is similar to when Stayman is doubled. The focus is on a stopper in the doubled suit.

3.5.1. With four-card support

Our super-accept scheme is ON, except that opener may use a reverse transfer to make responder declare.

Anti-splinter super-accepts (showing values, four-card-support and minumum) neither confirm nor deny control. A subsequent 3NT by responder shows control of the transfer-suit and is a slam try with preference to declare.

3.6. Minor-suit transfers

 ●  2♠ = ♣, or balanced inv
  ➤  2NT = min   ➤  3♣ = max
 ●  2NT = ♦, any strength
  ➤  3♣ = pre-decline   ➤  3♦ = pre-accept (♦Qxx or better)

3.6.1. Rebids with strong hands

 ●  new-suit = shortness   ➤  opener assumes COG
 ●  4m = minorwood  ●  other jump-shift = exclusion  ●  4NT = slam inv.

A 3NT rebid is generally to play, but opener may continue with undisclosed extras following a previous-round min or pre-decline.

3.7. Three-level responses

 ●  3♣ = five-card Stayman
 ●  3♦ = 5-5 minors GF
  ➤  3♥ ⇒ ♣   ➤  3♠ ⇒ ♦   ➤  3NT = poor hand for minors
 ●  3♥,3♠ = stiff with three in other major

3.8. Interference over 1NT

3.8.1. Vs penalty double

 ●  pass = to play   ➤  Redbl by opener = 5-card suit
 ●  Redbl = ♣ or ♦
 ●  2♣ = majors   ➤   2♦ or Redbl = no pref 
 ●  2♦, 2♥ = transfers  ●  2♠ = 4 × ♠ + 6 × m  ●  2NT = ♣ + ♦  ●  3m = 6 × m + 4 × ♥

3.8.2. Vs natural 2C (single-suit)

 ●  dble = take-out   ➤  subsequent cue (3♣) requests stopper
 ●  other system ON

3.8.3. Vs artificial double or 2C

 ●  System ON  ●  double of 2♣ = Stayman

We can pass to discover an unspecified suit, then use a take-out double or Lebensohl.

We play our 2NT system

3.8.5. Vs 2D, 2H or 2S (any)

 ●  2M = to play   (except Michaels vs 5-cd suit)
 ●  3m = inv.  ●  3M = forcing  ●  3 cue-bid = Stayman, no stopper  ●  2NT = Lebensohl
