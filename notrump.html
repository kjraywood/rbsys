With four-card support

Modified  –  Jul 25, 2018 

The super-accept system is almost the same as in the uncontested auction.

Exception: Opener does not bid the trump suit without the ace or king of the doubled suit; thus allowing responder to declare.

With a clear max opener jump accepts with control in the transfer-suit, or makes a reverse transfer  without control.

A new suit showing values but less than a max still applies.

With values in the transfer-suit and 4-card support, opener bids 2NT.

Minor-suit transfers

 ●  2♠ = ♣, or balanced inv   ➤  2NT = min   ➤  3♣ = max
 ●  2NT = ♦, any strength   ➤  3♣ = pre-decline   ➤  3♦ = pre-accept (♦Qxx or better)

Rebids with strong hands

 ●  new-suit = shortness   ➤  opener assumes COG
 ●  4m = minorwood  ●  other jump-shift = exclusion  ●  4NT = slam inv.

A 3NT rebid is generally to play, but opener may continue with undisclosed extras following a previous-round min or pre-decline.

3-level responses

 ●  3♣ = five-card Stayman
 ●  3♦ = 5-5 minors GF   ➤  3♥ ⇒ ♣   ➤  3♠ ⇒ ♦   ➤  3NT = poor hand for minors
 ●  3♥,3♠ = stiff with three in other major

 ●  Redbl = ♣ or ♦
 ●  2♣ = majors   ➤   2♦ or Redbl = no pref 
 ●  2♦, 2♥ = transfers  ●  2♠ = 4 × ♠ + 6 × m  ●  2NT = ♣ + ♦  ●  3m = 6 × m + 4 × ♥

vs. natural 2C (single-suit)

 ●  dble = take-out   ➤  cue (3♣) on next-round requests stopper
 ●  other system ON

vs. artificial double or 2C

 ●  System ON  ●  double of 2♣ = Stayman

We can pass to discover an unspecified suit, then use a take-out double or Lebensohl.
