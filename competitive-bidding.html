Scrambling 2NT

Used to find the best fit when partner forces you to bid at the three-level and the partnership is unlikely to have game on power.

Applies when  ●  opps have bid to 2♥ or 2♠  ●  partner makes a take-out double  ●  RHO passes  ●  the auction is not defined as Lebensohl

In the similar auction (1♠)‑Dbl‑(2♠)‑?, scrambling-2NT is not needed because advancer is not forced to bid, and can use a responsive double to show moderate values with more than one place to play. So 2NT is Lebensohl, enabling advancer to distinguish competive from invitational single-suited hands.

Doubling cue-bids

They cue-bid, we double

We overcall, they cue-bid, advancer doubles

When their cue-bid shows a fit, then our double also show a fit.

(1♣)-1♠-(2♠)-Dbl = normal raise to 2S

(1♥)-1♠-(3♠)- Dbl = willing to sacrifice in 4S

Opener doubles their cue-bid raise

When opener doubles their (typically) fit-showing cue-bid, it shows a good hand with support for unbid suits. It is like a take-out double of their bid-and-raised suit.
