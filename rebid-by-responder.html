12.5. Rebidding after 1H–1S

 ●  2♦ rebid = extra strength or extra length   i.e.  it is not a minimum 5-4  ●  1NT rebid includes min 2-5-4-2   and 1-5-4-3

12.5.1. Two-way Checkback after both majors

With 4×♦ and a minimum, opener rebids 1NT; not 2♦. Following 2♣ by responder, opener shows this hand by bidding 2♥ (paradox) instead of accepting the relay. It is safe because if responder had intended to drop the auction in 2♦, then we have at least a nine-card ♦-fit. Opener will have at most three weak spades, because opener would usually have raised to 2♠ with a minimum 3‑5‑4‑1.

12.7. Fourth suit on 3rd-round or later

  Fourth-Suit Auctions     –  Richard Pavlicek

Shows ace or a singleton when:
 ●  4th suit is bid for the 1st time   on 3rd round or later
 ●  auction is GF  ●  NT has not been bid  ●  applies to either player

If partner bids 3NT, fourth-suit bidder pulls with a singleton.

13.2. In comp.

When responder has shown a non-bust, e.g.  a 1-level response or negative double, then a reverse in comp. is forcing for one round. It does not promise a rebid as it does in an uncontested auction.

This follows the usual style of limit bidding in competition  when there’s a cue-bid available to establish a GF.

When opener has shown a three-card raise, responder may suggest an alternate trump suit in one of the minors. Following that, opener’s weakest bid is returning to 4M. Anything else is encouraging, usually indicating that the short-suit is a singleton.
