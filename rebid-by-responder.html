2wCB after both majors

With 4×♦ and a minimum, opener rebids 1NT; not 2♦. Following 2♣ by responder, opener shows this hand by bidding 2♥ (paradox) instead of accepting the relay. It is safe because if responder had intended to drop the auction in 2♦, then we have at least a nine-card ♦-fit. It denies three spades because opener would have raised to 2♠ with a minimum 3‑5‑4‑1.

In comp.

When responder has shown a non-bust, e.g.  a 1-level response or negative double, then a reverse in comp. is forcing for one round. It does not promise a rebid as it does in an uncontested auction.

This follows the usual style of limit bidding in competition  when there’s a cue-bid available to establish a GF.

3-card raise, short om

When opener has identified shortness and a three-card raise, responder may continue to investigate further, possibly suggesting an alternate trump suit in one of the minors. Following that, opener’s weakest bid is returning to 4M. Anything else is encouraging, usually indicating that the short-suit is a singleton.
