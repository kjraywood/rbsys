10. Vs Two-Suited Overcalls

Updated – Mar 11, 2020

With both suits shown by intervenor responder, responder passes and then doubles on the next round.

So pass then double is penalty.

10.1. Negative double

If they show two known suits, double shows four or five cards in the fourth suit and the values to compete. Without a fit for opener, responder will usually be able to penalise at least one of their suits.

If they show only one known suit, then doubles shows the other two suits

1♥ (2NT)⒜ X : 4 or 5 spades, 9+ HCP ⒜ both minors

1♥ (2H)⒝ X : both minors, 9+ HCP ⒝ ♠ + minor

Whether they showed one or two suits, a subsequent double by either player is penalty.

A negative double sets up a forcing pass below 2NT

This is because it requires four trumps to double them at the 2-level but double of a 3-level contract requires only a decent three-card suit.

Applies when they have shown two known suits
